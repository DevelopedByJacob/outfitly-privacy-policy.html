Privacy Policy for Outlify

Effective date: June 15, 2026

Outlify is developed and published by developedbyjacob.

This Privacy Policy explains how Outlify handles information when you use the app. By using Outlify, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

1. Information We Collect

Outlify is designed to help you organize outfits, clothing items and personal profiles on your device.

The app may access or store the following types of information:

Photos and images

Outlify allows you to add photos of clothing items, outfits or related content. You may add photos by taking a picture with your device camera or by selecting an existing image from your device gallery.

These photos are used only to provide the app’s core functionality, such as displaying and organizing your wardrobe and outfits.

Unless explicitly stated otherwise inside the app, photos added to Outlify are stored locally on your device and are not uploaded to servers controlled by developedbyjacob.

App profiles and outfit data

Outlify may allow you to create multiple local profiles, especially for Premium users. These profiles may include names, clothing items, outfit information, preferences and associated photos.

This information is used only to organize your content inside the app.

Purchase information

Outlify may offer a Premium version or in-app purchase to remove ads and unlock additional features, such as multiple profiles.

Payments and purchases are processed by Google Play. developedbyjacob does not receive or store your full payment details, such as credit card numbers or billing information.

The app may receive basic purchase status information from Google Play in order to enable Premium features.

Advertising data

The free version of Outlify may display banner ads using Google AdMob.

AdMob and its partners may collect and process certain information from your device, such as advertising identifiers, device information, approximate location, app interaction data and other data used to provide, limit, measure or personalize ads, depending on your consent choices and applicable laws.

You can learn more about how Google uses data for advertising in Google’s Privacy Policy and Google’s advertising information pages.

2. How We Use Information

Outlify uses information for the following purposes:

  • To provide the app’s main wardrobe and outfit organization features.
  • To let you add, view and manage clothing photos.
  • To support multiple local profiles when available.
  • To process and verify Premium access through Google Play.
  • To show banner ads in the free version of the app.
  • To remove ads for Premium users.
  • To improve app stability, functionality and user experience.

3. Sharing of Information

developedbyjacob does not sell your personal information.

Outlify may share limited data with third-party service providers only when necessary for app functionality, advertising, purchase processing or legal compliance.

The main third-party services that may process data are:

Google AdMob

Used to display banner ads in the free version of the app. Google AdMob may collect and process advertising-related data according to Google’s own privacy practices and your consent choices.

Google Play Billing

Used to process Premium purchases. Payments are handled by Google Play, not directly by developedbyjacob.

4. Camera and Photo Permissions

Outlify may request permission to access your camera or photo library.

These permissions are used only so you can take photos or select images to add to your wardrobe, outfits or profiles.

You can deny or revoke these permissions at any time through your device settings. Some app features may not work correctly without these permissions.

5. Local Storage

Outlify may store app content locally on your device, including photos, clothing items, outfit information, settings and profiles.

If you delete the app, locally stored data may also be deleted from your device, depending on your device settings and backup configuration.

At this time, Outlify does not provide its own cloud account system or server-based synchronization.

6. Advertising and Consent

Outlify may show ads through Google AdMob in the free version of the app.

Where required by law, especially in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom and Switzerland, the app may ask for your consent before showing personalized ads or using certain advertising-related technologies.

You may be able to manage your ad preferences through the consent form shown in the app, through your device settings, or through Google’s advertising settings.

Premium users may remove ads from the app after a valid purchase.

7. Children’s Privacy

Outlify is not specifically directed to children under the age of 13.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe that a child has provided personal information through the app, please contact us so we can review the situation and take appropriate action.

8. Data Security

We take reasonable measures to protect the information handled by Outlify.

However, no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure. You are responsible for keeping your device protected and for managing access to your photos and app data.

9. Data Retention and Deletion

Information stored locally in Outlify remains on your device until you delete it manually or uninstall the app.

You can delete clothing items, outfits, profiles or photos inside the app where such options are available.

You may also delete the app from your device to remove locally stored app data, subject to your device’s backup and storage settings.

10. Third-Party Services

Outlify may use third-party services that have their own privacy policies, including:

  • Google AdMob
  • Google Play services
  • Google Play Billing

We recommend reviewing Google’s privacy practices to understand how Google may process data through these services.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time.

Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. You are encouraged to review this Privacy Policy periodically.
